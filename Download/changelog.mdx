# Version 1.10.0

Download the newest available version of the X-ray Simulator on the [download page](/Download/download)

---

## New Tibia and Fibula Module

A dedicated Tibia and Fibula module is now available for long-bone positioning practice. It includes AP and lateral projection workflows, new patient assets, projection details, and optimal image references.

Students can access the module through the region selector.

![Tibia and Fibula module](/Assets/version110/tibia-fibula-module-framed.svg)

![Tibia and Fibula positioning](/Assets/version110/tibia-fibula-positioning-framed.svg)

## Digital X-ray Workflow

Version 1.10.0 adds digital X-ray workflow alongside the existing raw data workflow.

Users can now switch between digital image output and raw data output.

Digital images show EI, target EI, and DI. This gives students a more clinical review workflow and supports faculty discussions about exposure evaluation.

![Digital X-ray workflow](/Assets/version110/digital-xray-workflow-framed.svg)

## Image Review and Post-processing Tools

Desktop and VR workflows now include expanded tools for digital image review:
- Histogram views.
- Window width and window level controls.

Note: Some digital X-ray and post-processing tools may vary by installation depending on release configuration.

![Image review and post-processing tools](/Assets/version110/image-review-tools-framed.svg)

## Saved Projection Improvements

Saved projections now preserve more of the image and room state, including raw/digital mode, windowing settings, L/R ruler placement, mobile plate holder state, and app version metadata.

This helps saved cases load more consistently across faculty demonstrations and student practice sessions. This release also includes compatibility handling for older shoulder saved projections from version 1.9.0.

## Stability and Usability Improvements

Desktop X-ray rendering has been updated to reduce unnecessary image regeneration when the scene is stable. This helps prevent idle image noise, especially at low mAs.

Other improvements include keyboard support during login in the VR X-ray Simulator.

## VitaSim Launcher Kiosk Mode

Version 1.10.0 adds the VitaSim Launcher for VR training stations configured as Windows kiosk stations. The launcher walks students through Meta Link, headset connection, and Enable Link setup checks before starting the simulator.

Staff can use the launcher maintenance bar for support, restart, shutdown, and controlled kiosk exit during station management.

![VitaSim Launcher kiosk screen](/Assets/launcher/vitasim-launcher-preview.png)

## Room and Scene Updates

This release includes additional room and scene improvements for plate handling, table bucky behavior, mobile plate holder behavior, patient positioning, C-spine and skull bones showing through the clothing, shoulder skin coverage, and collimation display behavior.

Directional arrows now visibly show which way movable room controls can be adjusted, making it easier for students to understand the available movement directions before they interact with the controls.

![Room and scene updates](/Assets/version110/room-scene-updates-framed.svg)

![Movement direction arrows](/Assets/version110/movement-direction-arrows-framed.svg)

# Version 1.9.0

Download the newest version of the X-ray Simulator on the [download page](/Download/download)

---

## Updated Room Layout for Easier Workflow

We have redesigned the room layout and removed the door to improve movement between the examination and control rooms.
Additionally, the room has been expanded to accommodate the new Detenting and Bucky features introduced in this release.

![New Regions](/Assets/version190/Room_layout.png)


## Table and Wall Bucky Support for More Realistic Exams

We are excited to introduce support for table and wall bucky in the X-ray Simulator, enhancing the realism of student exams.
Key features include:
- Pull the bucky out using the main handles, and adjust it up/down or side/side with the knob handle.
- The bucky automatically snaps in and out for ease of use.
- The plate can only be positioned at the center, but its orientation affects how it sits in the bucky. Use this to align the long axis of the plate when needed.

![New Regions](/Assets/version190/Bucky.png)


## X-ray Tube Detenting to Wall Bucky and Radiographic Table
With the introduction of the bucky feature, we've added the ability to detent the X-ray tube to the center of the wall bucky or radiographic table. This mimics real-life beam alignment challenges.
How to use:
- Activate detenting with the new toggle button at the top left.
- Move the tube into position until you hear a click; detenting is confirmed by a colored light on the tube's front.
- Click the toggle button again to release the detent.

![New Regions](/Assets/version190/Detenting.png)


## Guiding Laser Toggle for Plate Centering in the Bucky
To assist with centering the central ray on the hidden plate inside the bucky, we’ve added a guiding laser on the virtual X-ray Tube.
Instructions:
- Turn on the laser using the new button located between the collimation dials.
- Click the button again to turn off the laser.
- Note: The collimation field and laser are controlled separately.

![New Regions](/Assets/version190/Laser.png)


## New Joint Mobilities for Improved Simulation Training

We’ve added new joint mobilities in select simulations to enhance training realism:
- The simulated cross-table knee exam now supports adduction and abduction, helping students align femur condyles in lateral images.
- The axis hip exam features a mobility that allows the raised leg to be pulled out and up, clearing axial images of overlapping thigh muscle mass.
- The shoulder simulation now enables arm rotation inward and outward, giving students more control for humerus alignment.

New joint mobilities can be adjusted by grabbing the highlighted regions.

![New Regions](/Assets/version190/New_ROM.png)

## New Standing Patient Simulation for Pelvic/Hip Case
Due to popular demand, a standing hip simulation is now available, reflecting common examination approaches in some countries.
You can find the new simulation under the existing hip/pelvic case by selecting the "standing" patient option.

![New Regions](/Assets/version190/Standing_hip.png)

## Core Improvements

### 🖥️ Desktop X-ray Simulator

- **Redesigned Region Selection**: 
  ```
  Region → Exam → Simulation
  ```
  This update simplifies navigation and aligns with the categorization used in most RadTech positioning books.
  ![Updated desktop navigation](/Assets/New_choose_region.jpg)

- **Control Panel Overhaul**:
  - Cleaner layout with more intuitive grouping.
  - The new **View Controls** panel allows you to toggle "collimation mode" via the grid-icon UI.
  - The Technique panel now shows an **estimate of the collimation cut-off size**.
  - Tube positioning controls now show an estimate of SID based on tube position and angle.
  ![New control panel](/Assets/New_controls.jpg)

- **New Functionalities**:
  - Users can now toggle skin visibility on the patient model.
  - Users can now adjust bed height via a slider for improved positioning practice.

- **Patient Movement**:
  - The patient can now be translated either in the horizontal plane (for standing) or the frontal plane (for supine).
