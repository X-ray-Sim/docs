The foot module uses free plating with the patient recumbent on the table and includes lower-leg, tarsal, metatarsal, and phalangeal anatomy.

![Foot simulator preview](/Assets/simulation-catalog/v1.9.0/foot-preview.jpg)

![Foot example exposure](/Assets/simulation-catalog/v1.9.0/foot-exposure.jpg)

> **Notice:** The example exposure demonstrates available anatomy only. It is not intended to represent a specific view, ideal standard, or diagnostic-quality image. Exposure results vary with tube positioning, technical factors, and patient alignment.

## Imaging capabilities

| Capability | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Radiographic tube | Full range of motion, including vertical, horizontal, tilt, and rotation. The cassette can be rotated, and collimation size is adjustable. |
| Detector methods | The detector is set up for free plating because the patient is recumbent on the table. |
| Simulated exposures | Confirmed support for AP, oblique, and lateral views. Additional views may be possible depending on patient range of motion and available bones. |

## Anatomy and movement

| Area | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Included anatomy | Tibia, fibula, tarsal bones, metatarsal bones, and phalanges. |
| Joint mobility | Foot plantarflexion and dorsiflexion for lateral body position. Hip internal and external rotation for lateral body position. Patient craniocaudal and mediolateral adjustment. |

## Bone variations

- Healthy bone version
- Pathological version featuring a 5th metatarsal avulsion fracture

## Known limitations

No known limitations for this simulation as of version v1.9.0.
